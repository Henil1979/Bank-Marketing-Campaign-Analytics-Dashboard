# Bank Marketing Campaign Analytics

## Overview

This project analyses the effectiveness of direct marketing campaigns conducted by a banking institution to increase term deposit subscriptions.

The objective was to identify customer characteristics, campaign factors, and behavioural patterns that influence subscription decisions. Using exploratory data analysis, data visualisation, and business intelligence techniques, the project provides actionable recommendations to improve future marketing performance and customer targeting.

## Business Problem

Banks invest significant resources into outbound marketing campaigns, but not all customer segments respond equally.

The business needed answers to the following questions:

* Which customer groups are most likely to subscribe to a term deposit?
* Which demographic characteristics influence subscription behaviour?
* How does campaign engagement impact conversion rates?
* Which customer segments should be prioritised for future campaigns?
* How can marketing resources be allocated more effectively?

## Dataset

The dataset contained customer demographic information, financial attributes, and campaign interaction data including:

* Age
* Job
* Marital Status
* Education
* Balance
* Housing Loan Status
* Personal Loan Status
* Contact Method
* Campaign Duration
* Previous Campaign Outcomes
* Subscription Outcome

## Key Insights

* Students and retirees demonstrated higher subscription rates than several other customer groups.
* Longer customer call durations were strongly associated with successful subscription outcomes.
* Customer demographics significantly influenced campaign effectiveness.
* Certain customer segments consistently showed lower conversion rates, indicating opportunities for more targeted marketing.
* Previous campaign outcomes provided useful indicators of future campaign success.

## Business Recommendations

### 1. Prioritise High-Converting Customer Segments

Focus future campaigns on customer groups with historically higher subscription rates, particularly students and retirees.

### 2. Improve Agent Engagement

Provide additional training to call agents to encourage meaningful customer conversations, as longer interactions were associated with higher conversion rates.

### 3. Develop Segment-Specific Campaigns

Create tailored messaging and offers based on occupation, age group, and customer profile.

### 4. Implement Age-Based Marketing Strategies

Use different communication approaches for younger and older customers based on behavioural patterns identified in the analysis.

### 5. Optimise Campaign Timing and Follow-Ups

Use historical campaign performance data to identify the most effective contact frequency and timing.

## Outcome

This project demonstrated how data analytics and business intelligence can be used to improve marketing efficiency, increase conversion rates, and support data-driven decision-making within the banking sector.
